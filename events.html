Showcase Your Excellence

Guidelines for Crafting a
Compelling Tenure Presentation

The teams are expected to prepare a presentation on the social work they have conducted, focusing on its impact on the community. The presentation should include the motivation behind organizing the social event, detailing the reasons and objectives that inspired the initiative, the key aspects of its execution, and the impact on society, highlighting the positive outcomes and long-term benefits of the project.

Guidelines for Tenure Presentation

  • Structure: Organize your presentation to provide clear information regarding the social work done.
  • Visuals: Incorporate photos, charts, or short videos to make the presentation engaging.
  • Team Representation: Presentations must be delivered by registered NSS Unit members.
  • Language: Presentations can be in English or Hindi/Marathi (depending on comfort), but must remain clear and concise
  • PPT Format: Use of PowerPoint/PPT is compulsory for clarity and professionalism.
  • PPT Template: Download the template

  • Number of presenters

    Each presenting team should consist of 2 members from team of 4. NSS unit Only.

  • Duration of Presentation

    Your presentation must be concluded within 10 minutes, followed by a Q&A session.

  • Duration of Video

    If including videos, ensure they are no longer than 2 minutes and directly relevant to your presentation.

What is Round-Table Discussion?

Round-Table Discussion is a collaborative exercise where participants discuss a specific topic to evaluate their ability to communicate, collaborate, and present their ideas effectively within a group setting. It is often used in academic and professional settings to gauge participants' ability to articulate their thoughts and engage in constructive dialogue.

Innovate for Social Change

Guidelines for Participating in the
Ideathon (Social Track)

The Ideathon (Social Track) challenges NSS participants to design innovative and practical solutions for real-world community problems. Given a problem statement, teams will pitch their ideas in a startup-style format, focusing on creativity, feasibility, and sustainable social impact.

Guidelines for Ideathon Participation

  • Team Size: 1–4 members per team.
  • Eligibility: Open to all NSS volunteers from participating units and VIT college students.
  • Problem Statements: Domain-wise social problem statements will be provided
  • Structure:PPT Submission: Teams submit their solution.
  • Presentation Format: PPT with visuals, diagrams, or mock-ups

  • Team Composition

    Each team must consist of 1–4 members. All members may participate in presenting the idea.

  • Presentation Rounds

    PPT Submission.

Engineering Ideas into Reality

Innovation and Prototyping at
Sociothon (Technical Track)

What is Sociothon?

The Sociothon – (Technical Track) is a team-based competition where students tackle real-world technical problem statements and present innovative solutions. It provides a platform to pitch ideas, develop prototypes, and showcase creativity, technical expertise, and teamwork, with recognition and prizes for the best solutions.

Structure & Rounds

  • Round 1 – Online PPT Submission round (Free) No charges
  • Round 2 – Offline Need to register on unstop Shortlisted Teams only

Rules & Guidelines

  • Prize Pool: ₹20,000
  • Team Size: 2-4 members per team.
  • Eligibility: Open to all B.E./B.Tech students (First Year to Final Year). Cross-college teams are not permitted.
  • Winners: Top 3 teams will be awarded.
  • Problem Statements: Provided on the Unstop platform.
  • Requirements: Participants must bring their College ID and Aadhar card for verification. Certificates will be provided to all attendees.

Celebrate Heritage through Art

Guidelines for Participating in
Kala Kriti

Kala Kriti is a competition that showcases the richness of Indian classical dance and music. It provides a platform for young artists to display their talent, creativity, and passion for our cultural heritage.

Guidelines for Kala Kriti

  • Participants: Open only to VIT College Students.
  • Categories: Classical Dance (Solo & Group).
    Classical Vocal & Instrumental Music.

  • Eligibility

    Participation is restricted to VIT College Students only.

  • Competition Categories

    Classical Dance: Solo & Group performances.
    Music: Classical Vocal & Instrumental.

  • Focus

    Emphasis on talent, creativity, and cultural heritage through classical art forms.
